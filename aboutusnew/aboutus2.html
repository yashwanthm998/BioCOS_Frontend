Solutions Offered

Sequencing

Sequencing DNA means determining the order of the four chemical building blocks - called "bases" - that make up the DNA molecule. The sequence tells scientists the kind of genetic information that is carried in a particular DNA segment.

Read More

Genotyping

Genotyping is the process of determining the DNA sequence, called a genotype, at positions within the genome of an individual. Sequence variations can be used as markers in linkage and association studies to determine genes relevant to specific traits.

Read More

Microarray

A microarray is a laboratory tool used to detect the expression of thousands of genes at the same time. DNA microarrays are microscope slides that are printed with thousands of tiny spots in defined positions, with each spot containing a known DNA sequence or gene.

Read More

Bioinformatics

a hybrid science that links biological data with techniques for information storage, distribution, and analysis to support multiple areas of scientific research, including biomedicine. Bioinformatics focuses its scope on the areas of 3D image processing and many more.
